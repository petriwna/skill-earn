FAQ

  • Do I need to register a business to work as a freelancer in Canada?

    You don’t always need to register a business if you're working as a sole proprietor using your legal name. However, if you operate under a business name or want to issue invoices, it's recommended to register with your provincial business registry.

  • Can I legally work online in Canada with a PR or Work Permit?

    Yes. Permanent Residents (PR) and valid Work Permit holders can earn income through freelancing, remote jobs, or running an online business — as long as it complies with immigration and tax rules.

  • How do I report freelance or online income to the CRA?

    Freelance and online income is considered self-employment income in Canada. You must report it annually via your T1 tax return using the T2125 form (Statement of Business or Professional Activities).

  • Are freelance platforms like Fiverr or Upwork legal in Canada?

    Yes. These platforms are legal and commonly used by Canadians. Income from such platforms must be reported to the CRA.
